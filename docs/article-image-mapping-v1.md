# Second Season — Article Hero Image Mapping (v1)

A practical guide for assigning **one dedicated hero image per article**. Use this when selecting or commissioning photography from `public/images/editorial/`.

**Rule:** No two articles should share the same visual theme. If two images could sit on the same mood board, one of them is wrong.

**Scope:** All 10 currently published articles (Issue #001 + Issue #002). The seven articles listed in the original brief appear first; three additional Issue #002 articles follow with the same standard.

---

## How to use this document

1. Read **Uniqueness at a glance** before approving any new hero.
2. For each article, shoot or select against **ideal subject** — not against category alone.
3. Check **avoid subjects** and **uniqueness notes** against neighboring articles in the same category (Essay, Places, Journeys, etc.).
4. Save files under the recommended editorial folder using the suggested filename pattern.

## Issue #001 & core articles

### 1. 35년 교직 생활 이후

**Slug:** `after-35-years-teaching`  
**Site category:** Essay  
**Editorial folder:** `books/` (secondary: `home/`)

**Emotional tone:** Quiet relief after a long chapter. Not celebration — a first morning that belongs entirely to oneself. Thoughtful, unhurried, slightly literary.

**Ideal subject:**  
Open book on a wooden desk, reading glasses resting beside it, warm **morning** window light. Empty chair implied. Desk shows use — not a showroom study.

**Avoid subjects:**
- Classroom, blackboard, or school imagery
- Retirement party, champagne, or “freedom” clichés
- Generic senior portrait or smiling stock model
- Night reading (belongs to a different emotional register)
- Book stacks (reserved for *나이가 들수록 좋아지는 것들*)

**Image uniqueness notes:**  
This is the **only** article whose hero centers on a **single open book + glasses on a desk**. Do not reuse for any essay about aging, friendship, or morning routine. The light must read as *morning*, not afternoon café light.

**Suggested filename:** `books-morning-desk-glasses.jpg`

### 2. 좋은 장소를 알아보는 기준

**Slug:** `how-to-know-a-good-place`  
**Site category:** Places  
**Editorial folder:** `travel/` (secondary: `window/`)

**Emotional tone:** Calm judgment. A place you would walk again without planning. Evening ease, not discovery hype.

**Ideal subject:**  
Quiet **city street at dusk** — soft streetlights, empty or sparse sidewalk, residential or small-shop scale. Shot at walking height, as if pausing mid-stroll. No landmark signage.

**Avoid subjects:**
- Tourist landmarks or “must visit” icons
- Crowded commercial districts
- Interior café scenes (reserved for *햇살이 오래 머무는 카페*)
- Beach or coastline (reserved for journey articles)
- Instagram-perfect alley murals

**Image uniqueness notes:**  
The hero is **urban exterior at dusk, on foot**. No other article uses a neighborhood **street** as its primary theme. Differs from *제주의 오후* (rural stone wall) and from all sea/travel journey shots.

**Suggested filename:** `travel-neighborhood-street-dusk.jpg`

### 3. 햇살이 오래 머무는 카페

**Slug:** `cafe-where-sunlight-lingers`  
**Site category:** Places  
**Editorial folder:** `cafes/` (secondary: `window/`)

**Emotional tone:** Afternoon stillness. Time slows inside. Light stays on the table longer than expected.

**Ideal subject:**  
Small café **interior**, window seat, **afternoon** sun falling across an empty or single-cup table. Plants, worn wood, low noise. View may hint at a alley or garden — not open sea.

**Avoid subjects:**
- Latte art hero shots
- Trendy third-wave branding or industrial chrome
- Beach-view café (too close to *제주의 오후*)
- Two place settings (reserved for *오래된 친구와의 점심*)
- Morning light (reserved for *35년 교직 생활 이후* and *아침이 좋아진 이유*)

**Image uniqueness notes:**  
The **only** article whose hero is **café interior + lingering window light**. Must not read as generic “coffee shop stock.” Interior, not street; afternoon, not morning.

**Suggested filename:** `cafes-window-seat-afternoon.jpg`

### 4. 강릉에서 보낸 이틀

**Slug:** `two-days-in-gangneung`  
**Site category:** Journeys  
**Editorial folder:** `sea/` (secondary: `travel/`)

**Emotional tone:** Slow coastal travel. Memory of walking, not sightseeing. Gentle repetition of waves.

**Ideal subject:**  
**Seaside walk** — empty path or wooden steps beside **calm** water, early morning or soft overcast. Horizon present but not dramatic. Sense of **movement along** the shore, not sitting still.

**Avoid subjects:**
- ❌ Generic **beach** hero (blank sand, postcard horizon) — too close to wrong patterns
- ❌ Same static shoreline used for *제주의 오후*
- Surf, crowds, or summer resort energy
- Café window with ocean view
- Famous Gangneung landmarks as the subject

**Image uniqueness notes:**  
**Bad:** 강릉 → beach | 제주 → beach (same theme)  
**Good:** 강릉 → **seaside walk** (path, motion, east-coast quiet) | 제주 → **stone wall and afternoon light** (stillness, village texture)

This article owns **horizontal movement beside water**. *제주의 오후* owns **still afternoon light on stone/wall** — not open beach.

**Suggested filename:** `sea-shore-path-morning-walk.jpg`

### 5. 나이가 들수록 좋아지는 것들

**Slug:** `things-that-get-better-with-age`  
**Site category:** Essay  
**Editorial folder:** `books/` (secondary: `home/`)

**Emotional tone:** Accumulation and depth. Simple things improving with time. Gratitude without sentimentality.

**Ideal subject:**  
**Stack of well-loved books** on a wooden shelf — varied spines, slight wear, faded covers. No single open book (that belongs to *35년 교직 생활 이후*). Optional: edge of armchair or side table in frame.

**Avoid subjects:**
- Open book on desk with glasses
- Garden or seasonal imagery (*계절이 보이는 정원*)
- Meal or restaurant tables
- Faces or “happy senior” lifestyle models
- Minimalist empty shelf — needs evidence of **years of reading**

**Image uniqueness notes:**  
The **only** hero built around **multiple books as accumulated time**. Pairs with *35년 교직 생활 이후* (one open book, one moment) but must never duplicate it.

**Suggested filename:** `books-worn-stack-shelf.jpg`

### 6. 제주의 오후

**Slug:** `an-afternoon-in-jeju`  
**Site category:** Journeys  
**Editorial folder:** `travel/` (secondary: `window/`)

**Emotional tone:** One long afternoon. A single scene remembered. Stillness over itinerary.

**Ideal subject:**  
**Stone wall and afternoon light** — Jeju volcanic stone, low wall or courtyard edge, warm slanting sun, perhaps wild grass or a quiet lane. Optionally: simple window frame with sea **glimpse** in background — but wall/light is the lead, not open water.

**Avoid subjects:**
- ❌ Open **beach** or wide seascape hero (conflicts with *강릉에서 보낸 이틀*)
- Full café interior (*햇살이 오래 머무는 카페*)
- Tourism landmarks (Oreum hero shots, lighthouse postcards)
- Dramatic sunset saturation

**Image uniqueness notes:**  
**Bad:** beach for both east-coast and Jeju journey articles  
**Good:** Gangneung = **walk beside water** | Jeju = **stone, wall, afternoon stillness**

This article owns **textured stillness + afternoon gold on stone**. Not a path, not a horizon-first composition.

**Suggested filename:** `travel-jeju-stone-wall-afternoon.jpg`

### 7. 오래된 친구와의 점심

**Slug:** `lunch-with-an-old-friend`  
**Site category:** Essay  
**Editorial folder:** `meals/` (secondary: `window/`)

**Emotional tone:** Warm familiarity. Conversation without performance. Trust built over decades.

**Ideal subject:**  
**Two place settings** at a modest table — simple dishes, soft daylight, worn table surface. Chairs angled as if two people just sat down or paused mid-meal. **No faces.**

**Avoid subjects:**
- Single diner or solo table (*다시 찾게 되는 작은 식당*)
- Fine dining or white-tablecloth formality
- Restaurant exterior or storefront
- Café coffee-only scene
- Overhead food-styling flat-lay

**Image uniqueness notes:**  
The **only** hero explicitly showing **two settings / shared meal for two**. *다시 찾게 되는 작은 식당* uses a **single regular’s corner** — one relationship to a place, not two people at lunch.

**Suggested filename:** `meals-two-settings-daylight.jpg`

### 9. 다시 찾게 되는 작은 식당

**Slug:** `small-restaurant-worth-returning-to`  
**Site category:** Places  
**Editorial folder:** `meals/` (secondary: `home/`)

**Emotional tone:** Loyalty to a humble place. Honest food, familiar corner. Return visit, not first discovery.

**Ideal subject:**

**Image uniqueness notes:**  
Owns **one regular’s table in a small restaurant** — relationship to a **place**, not a **person**. Pairs with lunch article (two people) and café article (afternoon light) without overlap.

## Cross-check before publishing a new hero

Ask these five questions:

1. **Could this image work for another article on this list?** If yes, reject or re-shoot.
2. **Is the time of day correct?** Morning desk ≠ afternoon café ≠ dusk street.
3. **Is the scale correct?** Interior vs exterior vs path vs wall.
4. **Is human presence implied correctly?** Zero, one regular, or two friends — never mixed up.
5. **Does it feel like a thoughtful observer, not a brand?** If it sells something, it fails.
